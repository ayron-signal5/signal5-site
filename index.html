Critical Setup Checklist

Six points to an airtight setup.

Every client goes through this checklist with our team before going live. These are the six elements we verify together — no guesswork, no assumptions.

01

Factory Reset Your Device

Before onboarding, your iPhone must be fully factory reset. This eliminates residual device signals, cached location data, and prior platform fingerprints that could conflict with your new US identity.

Settings → General → Transfer or Reset → Erase All Content
02

No Physical SIM

Your device must carry no European SIM while operating in US mode. A resident SIM leaks your real carrier identity at the baseband level — invisible to apps but detectable to platform infrastructure.

SIM slot: empty or eSIM disabled
03

GPS & Location Services Off

System-level location must be disabled, not just withheld from individual apps. Any GPS ping cross-referenced against your carrier IP creates a detectable inconsistency.

Settings → Privacy → Location: Disabled
04

Private US DNS

DNS queries must resolve through US-based resolvers, not your European ISP. DNS leaks are one of the most common failure points — betraying your geographic origin even when everything else is correct.

DNS: US resolver · Leak test: Pass
05

System-Level Kill-Switch

Your device should route zero traffic if the carrier connection drops, even momentarily. App-level kill-switches are insufficient — the protection must be enforced at the operating system network layer.

Network layer: enforced · App fallback: none
06

US-Only App Environment

The App Store region, Apple ID, and all associated accounts must reflect a US identity. A US carrier IP paired with a European App Store account is a signal inconsistency. We audit the full chain — not just the network layer — and walk you through any corrections on a live call.

App Store: US · Apple ID region: US · Payment method: US-configured
